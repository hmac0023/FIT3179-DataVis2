The rise of obesity in Australia

Number of Overweight and Obese Individuals In Australia (2007-2022)

Over time, the number of overweight and obese individuals in Australia has shown a significant upward trend. This data highlights the growing public health challenge, with a steep rise over the past few decades.

Self-Assessed Health Distribution

In 2022, self-assessed health in Australia revealed that a significant portion of the population considers themselves to be in good or very good health. However, this does not necessarily align with actual obesity statistics, indicating potential gaps in self-awareness regarding health conditions.

Distribution of Body Mass Index (BMI)

The BMI distribution chart shows a substantial number of individuals in the overweight and obese categories, emphasizing the need for targeted interventions in these groups. A relatively small percentage fall into the underweight category.

Physical Activity Distribution

Physical activity levels in Australia suggest that a considerable portion of the population does not meet the recommended physical activity guidelines. This correlates with the rising obesity rates and emphasizes the importance of promoting regular exercise.

Overweight and Obese Individuals by State

There are significant geographic differences in the distribution of overweight and obese individuals across Australian states. This map highlights areas that may require more focused health initiatives and resources to combat obesity.
